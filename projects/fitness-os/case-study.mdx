<Section n="02" title="The Pitch">

**Problem.** Most of my systems show up here finished. This one doesn't — and that's the point. Training is a domain where I have real data flowing (workouts logged in Hevy, calories and macros logged in Fitbee, a weekly weigh-in) but no system tying it together yet. The honest move isn't to fake a polished case study; it's to put the half-built thing on the wall and let the changelog do the talking. Every other portfolio shows you the highlight reel. This page shows you the work in progress.

**System.** The blueprint is a standardization loop, built in stages: **ingest** the raw signals — workouts from a **Hevy** CSV export and nutrition actuals (calories + macros) from **Fitbee**, joined to the weekly weigh-in — then a **weekly programming loop** where the AI drafts next week's training from last week's *actuals* and a human approves it, a **monthly performance-review memo**, and a **quarterly program redesign**. Today only the early pieces exist; the rest is roadmap. The maturity score says 2 and means it — that's not an apology, it's the spec.

**Payoff.** Two things. First, the practical one: a training system that programs itself from what I actually did, not what some generic plan assumed. Second, the meta one — this is what "build in public" looks like done honestly. A portfolio that shows a `2/5` getting built, with visible velocity in the changelog, is more credible than one where everything is suspiciously finished. Immaturity, labeled and moving, becomes a reason to come back and check on it.

</Section>

<Section n="04" title="AI Architecture">

Three named roles, most of which are still on the roadmap — stated as the design, not a claim of current state:

<Roles roles={manifest.ai_roles} />

- **Ingest.** Normalizes the raw exports — the **Hevy** workout CSV and **Fitbee** nutrition log — into a clean weekly picture of actuals: what got trained (sets, loads, RPE), calories and protein actually logged, and the bodyweight trend from the weekly weigh-in. The foundation everything else reads from. *(v1 in definition.)*
- **Programmer.** The weekly draft engine: takes last week's actuals and proposes next week's training and macro targets. Deliberately a *draft* — it never auto-applies.
- **Reviewer.** Writes the monthly performance-review memo from the trend data and flags what to change heading into the next block.

**Where the human gate sits.** This is a heavily human-gated system by design, which fits a domain where the cost of a bad automated call is real fatigue or injury. Ingest and the memo draft can run automatically; **every programming and nutrition change is human-approve.** The AI proposes the week; the human accepts it.

*Data posture: this page surfaces **trends and adherence %**, never raw body data. No weights, no measurements, no raw health numbers — just whether the system is being followed and which direction the trend points.*

</Section>

<Section n="06" title="Challenges & Lessons">

- **Publishing it at a `2/5` is the harder choice, and the right one.** The instinct is to wait until it's impressive. But a portfolio whose only failure mode is "everything's finished" reads as a highlight reel, and senior readers discount highlight reels. Shipping a labeled, honestly-immature system — with the changelog as the proof of motion — is a credibility play, not a confession. Visible velocity on an unfinished system beats a static finished-looking one.
- **Start the loop deliberately small.** The temptation with fitness data is to ingest *everything* and build the grand programmer on day one. That's how this kind of project dies: too much surface, never ships. The v1 loop is being defined as the *smallest* thing that closes: pull the Hevy export + Fitbee actuals → draft a week → approve. Earn complexity later.
- **Privacy is a design constraint, not an afterthought.** Health data is the most sensitive thing any of my systems touch. The decision to surface only **trends and adherence %** — never raw body metrics — is baked into the architecture from the start, not bolted on before launch.
- **What I'd redo.** Nothing yet — that's the honest answer for a system this early, and saying so is more credible than inventing a war story. The "what I'd redo" will fill in as the loops actually run.

</Section>
